Time Trends in Liver Cancer Incidence by Race/Ethnicity and Cancer Stage, United States, 1992-2011

Summer 2014
I worked under the supervision of Dr. Chris Li at the Cancer Epidemiology Research Cooperative alongside Lu Chen. We used data from 13 population-based cancer registries participating in the National Cancer Registry's Surveillance, Epidemiology, and End Results (SEER) Program.

We found that liver cancer rates overall were steadily increasing for White and Black populations for localized, regional, and distant stage disease between 1992 and 2011. Although incidence rates for Asians/Pacific Islanders have plateaud in recent years, they remain the highest among all racial groups.

Cancer Prevention Strategies For Washington State

Summer 2013
I evaluated various cancer prevention strategies by considering the costs, benefits, and potential drawbacks ssociated with these measures. This was an exploratory project into both statistics and policy, in which I provided an overview of the burden of disease and an assessment of treatment costs for prevalent cancers.

I analyzed the impact of prevention tools on cancer prevalence and further provided a business case demonstrating incentive for investment in tobacco cessation, mammography, and colonoscopy.
